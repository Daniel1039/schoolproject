Alhaji (Chief) A.B. Olaboopo, a distinguished individual, was born into a devout Muslim family in Iragbiji, Osun State, located in the western part of Nigeria. His educational journey began in 1955 when he embarked on his primary school education. With a thirst for knowledge, he continued his studies at A.U.D College in Ota, where he obtained a Grade Two certificate in 1964-1965, laying a solid foundation for his academic pursuits. Driven by a passion for excellence, Alhaji (Chief) A.B. Olaboopo pursued his professional.
Expanding his intellectual horizons, he embarked on a new chapter of his educational journey at the prestigious University of Ibadan from 1984 to 1987. During this time, he delved into the study of Economics and earned an honorable Bachelor of Science degree, further enriching his understanding of economic principles fueling his intellectual curiosity.

Alhaji (Chief) A.B. Olaboopo's commitment to education and his vision for providing quality schooling led him to establish the Nurudeen Group of Schools in Iwo, Osun State. As the proprietor, he has created an educational institution that upholds the values of academic excellence, character development, and holistic growth. Through his dedication, leadership, and unwavering support, Alhaji (Chief) A.B. Olaboopo has played a pivotal role in shaping the educational landscape, providing countless students with opportunities to thrive academically and personally. His contributions to the field of education have left an indelible mark on the lives of many, empowering them to pursue their dreams and make a positive impact in their communities.
